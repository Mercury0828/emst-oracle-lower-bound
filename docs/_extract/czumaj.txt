1 Introduction
As the power and connectivity of computers increases, and
the cost of memory becomes cheaper, we have become
inundated with large amounts of data. Although traditionally
linear time algorithms were sought to solve our problems, it
is no longer clear that a linear time algorithm is good enough
in every setting. The question then is whether we can solve
anything of interest in sublinear time, when we are not even
given time to read all of the input data. In recent years,
several sublinear time algorithms have been presented which
solve a wide range of property testing and approximation
problems.
