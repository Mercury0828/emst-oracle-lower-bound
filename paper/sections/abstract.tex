We give a randomized algorithm that, for $n$ points in $[\Delta]^2$ with $\Delta=O(n)$, estimates the
weight of the Euclidean minimum spanning tree to within a $(1\pm\eps)$ factor using
$\Otil_\eps(n^{1/3})$ orthogonal range-counting queries. This matches the $\Omega(n^{1/3})$ lower bound
of Driemel et al.\ (SoCG 2025) up to polylogarithmic factors, resolving the range-counting query
complexity of Euclidean MST-weight estimation.
